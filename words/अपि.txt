A particle originally implying <i>location</i> and hence <i>proximity,</i> which, like other particles of location, then has become one implying <i>emphasis.</i> It is used, in some instances, as a prefix to verbs, is considered in one instance as a separable preposition governing a noun, but occurs more commonly as an adverb or conjunction. It forms with a following noun in a few instances Tatpur. and Bahuvr. compounds. Its older form is पि (see the Preface). <div n='P'>1. (as a <i>prefix to verbs</i>) <sup>1</sup> On; comp. e. g. धा, नह्, बन्ध्. (In this sense it answers the cognate German or English particle be-, as in beschlagen, bestreichen, beget, bespeak &c., and passes, as other locative prefixes, e. g. अधि, into the meaning of <i>much, very;</i> comp. e. g. गॄ with अपि, अपिगीर्ण.) Its older form पि, is met with esp. in this sense; comp. e. g. पिधान, पिनद्ध.--Before वृ, in forms in which this radical preserves its vowel ऋ, the final vowel of the prefix is lengthened in the Veda; comp. अपी- वृत; equally so in the क्विप् derivation अपीजु (from जू with अपि). [<sup>2</sup> In; in combination with अस् cl. 2. ‘to be’; in the Vedas(?). I. A.] <div n='P'>3. (as an <i>adverb</i> or <i>conjunction</i>) it implies emphasis, either by imparting greater power to <i>one</i> word or by producing a stronger junction or a stronger antithesis between <i>several</i> words or sentences; I. <i>by imparting greater power</i> <sup>A.</sup> to <i>one</i> word in general, esp. to <i>a noun;</i> it is synonymous then with एव and placed in most instances after the word to which it refers, but sometimes also after a word depending on, or relating to, that to which it properly belongs, to effectuate, as it were, at the same time a stronger relation between both; e. g. निष्ठुराश्लीलतीव्रत्वात्तदपि (i. e. वाक्पा- रुष्यं) त्रिविधं स्मृतम्; or जनानुरागेण युतोऽवसादः फलानुबन्धः सुधियात्मनोऽपि; or मुक्तबद्धयोरन्यतराभावान्न तत्सिद्धिः (scil. ईश्वरस्य) । उभयथाप्यसत्करत्वम्; or न्यग्जातयो रत्नवरैरलभ्या मुख्याः कपीनामपि नोपजप्याः (where अपि increases the power of मुख्याः, but produces at the same time in मुख्याः कपीनाम्, by being placed after the latter the effect of a stronger junction, like that of a compound कपिमुख्याः).In the same manner as the positive power of a word, if it implies, e. g. excellence &c., is rendered more prominent by अपि, also the negative value of a word implying negation, diminution &c. becomes still more negative &c. by the same particle; thus मुहुर्तमपि means ‘only a moment’, and न मुहुर्तमपि ‘not even a moment’; e. g. किं हि संशय- मापन्ने तस्मिन्निह मया तव । मुहूर्तमप्यजीवन्त्या यन्नान्वेष्यसि रा- घवम्; or नाहमेनं धनुष्पाणिं युयुत्सुं समुपस्थितम् । मुहूर्तमपि पश्येयं प्रहरेयं न चाप्युत; for the same reason it imparts to numerals the notion of <i>totality</i> or greater unity, e. g. द्वा- वपि ‘both two’, पञ्चापि ‘all five’ &c. <sup>B.</sup> To <i>verbs;</i> when imparting greater emphasis to the verbal action it is <sup>a.</sup> a particle of <i>interrogation,</i> although the verb would imply the same modality by itself alone, e. g. अपि भवान्कमण्डलु- पाणिं छात्रमद्राक्षीत्? or कुशलं कौशिको राज्ञः पर्यपृच्छत्सु- धार्मिकः । अपि ते संनताः सर्वे सामन्ता रिपवो जिताः? ॥. <sup>b.</sup> a particle of <i>exclamation;</i> <sup>α.</sup> in general, <i>he! ho there!</i> e. g. अपि विजहीहि दृढोपगूहनम्!; according to the <i>Gaṇaratnam.</i> it occurs combined with अयि, if the sentence is not completed, e. g. अय्यपि साहसकारिणि! <sup>β.</sup> more especially it may impart to the <i>imperative</i> the notion of <i>‘do as you please’</i> (<i>Pāṇ.</i> in the sense of अन्ववसर्ग, the same as कामकारक्रिया or क्रियाकारक्रिया of the Koshas), but rather with the implied meaning of ‘indifference’ than with that of a ‘kind permission’; e. g. (Sītā having told Rāvaṇa that her husband will entirely destroy him, continues: <i>Bhaṭṭik.</i> 8. 92.) अपि स्तुह्यपि सेधास्मांस्तथ्यमुक्तं नराशन ‘now do as you please: praise us (<i>comm.</i> say that I have spoken properly) or retain us: I have spoken the truth, man-eater!’; <sup>γ.</sup> to the <i>potential</i> or <i>conditional</i> (the natural value of which moods is uncertainty or insufficiency), it gives the notion of <i>‘certainty’</i> (‘वाढम्’) or of <i>‘exaggerated capacity’</i> (संभावने), then meaning <i>certainly!</i> <i>indeed!</i> e. g. अपि कुर्यात् or अप्यधीयीत ‘indeed he will do it, certainly he will study’; or अपि द्रोणपाकं भुञ्जीत ‘indeed he is capable to eat as much as a Drona full’; अपि सिञ्चेन्मू- लसहस्रम् ‘certainly he can water a thousand roots’; अपि साक्षात्प्रशिष्यास्त्वं कृच्छ्रेष्विन्द्रपुरोहितम् ‘(Rāvaṇa) thou art indeed capable of instructing the domestic priest of Indra (i. e. Vṛhaspati) when he is in need’; अपि तत्र रिपुः सीतां प्रार्थयि- ष्यत (conditional) दुर्मतिः ‘surely, because (Rāvaṇa) thy enemy is wicked, he desired for Sītā; (comm. उताप्योः समर्थयोरिति --<i>Pāṇ.</i> III. 3. 152.--। अत्रापि शब्दो वाढार्थः । लिङ्गिमि- त्तम् । तस्मिंँल्लिङ्गिमित्ते क्रियातिपत्तौ (comp. <i>Pāṇ.</i> III. 3. 139.) सत्यां भूते नित्यं ऌङ् । तत्र वोताप्योरित्यनुवर्वते); or it increases the original value of the potential by adding to it the notion of <i>fear</i> or <i>anxiety</i> (शङ्कायाम्), implying then <i>‘perhaps’</i> (‘शङ्कायाम्’); e. g. अपि चौरो भवेत् ‘there is perhaps a thief’; or of <i>‘hope’</i> (‘अपेक्षायाम्’), e. g. अपि गृह्लीयां वेदम् ‘I hope I mīght master the Veda’; if the potential is used in the sense of the imperative, it may add to it the notion of <i>‘contempt’</i> (गर्हा or निन्दा), e. g. ‘अपि स्तूयाद्वृषलम्’ ‘let him be so mean as to praise a Śūdra’; धिग्जाल्मं देवदत्तमपि सिञ्चेत्पलाण्डुम् ‘shame over the wretched Devadatta, let him go and water the onion’; अपि सिञ्चेः कृशानौ त्वं दर्पं मय्यपि योऽभिकः ‘go (Rāvaṇa) and throw thy manhood into the fire, as thou art (base enough) to make love to me’; <sup>δ.</sup> when combined with the <i>present</i> tense, it may impart to it the notion of ‘contempt’ (गर्हा) and give it the value of any other tense, e. g. अपि तत्र भवान्वृषलं याजयति ‘how wrong it is that thou makest a Śūdra perform a sacrifice’!; मन्त्रे जातु वदन्त्यज्ञास्त्वं ता- नप्यनुमन्यसे ‘fools sometimes tender advice; to those thou hast listened!’. <sup>C.</sup> To <i>interrogatives</i> (whether pronouns or interrogative particles); such words (implying doubt or uncertainty) assume with अपि the notion of <i>indefiniteness;</i> thus किमपि means ‘some one or some thing’, कुत्रापि or क्वापि ‘somewhere’, कुतोऽपि ‘from somewhere, somewhere’, कदापि ‘some time’, कथमपि ‘somehow’, when अपि may also be added with increasing power to the interrogatives which have become indefinites by means of चित्, e. g. किंचिदपि, कदाचिदपि &c. in the same but a more emphatic sense as किमपि, कदापि &c. <sup>D.</sup> To <i>particles</i> or <i>adverbs;</i> combined with them it increases their original power; thus चापि, एवापि, तथापि च, अपि चैव, अपि तु (but on the contrary), अप्युत, अप्येवम्, नापि (not even), अपि वा (more seldom वापि; e. g. in the <i>Jaimini Sūtra:</i> अपूर्वे वापि भागित्वात्; <i>Śabara:</i> वाअपीति विपर्यासेन प्रयुक्तौ । अपि वेत्यर्थः), भूयोऽपि, पुनरपि, अथवापि, अथापि, अथो अपि &c., have in most instances only the meaning of च, एव &c. but with greater emphasis.--अपि च occurs often for the purpose of connecting sentences and verses loosely connected, in the same sense as अन्यच्च (see s. v. अन्य 1. a.);
