The early Āryans must have dwelt in villages which were scattered over the country, some close together, some far apart, and were connected by roads.<sup>385</sup> In the early Vedic literature village is regularly contrasted with the forest (āraṇya) in the evening the cattle regularly returned thither from the forest.<sup>386</sup> The villages were probably open, though perhaps a fort(pur) might on occasion be built inside.<sup>387</sup> Presumably they consisted of detached houses with enclosures, but no details are to be found in Vedic literature. Large villages (mahāgrāmāḥ) were known.<sup>388</sup> The grāma may, however, perhaps be regarded more correctly as an aggregate of several families, not necessarily forming a clan, but only part of a clan (viś), as is often the case at the present day.<sup>389</sup> The head of the village was called Grāmaṇi or ‘the leader of the village’.
